About Us

The NetWIS Lab (Networking of Wireless Information Systems), led by Dr. Wenye Wang, is a research group dedicated to advancing the understanding and protocol design of mobile wireless networks. Our vision is to create resilient, high-performance systems for diverse applications and large-scale intelligent platforms. Through new models, experiments, and analysis of wireless networks in varied conditions, we design algorithms that support robust structures and reliable performance. Check out our publications page for more detail.

The lab brings together undergraduate students gaining their first research experience as preparation for graduate study, and graduate students pursuing innovative contributions to the field of wireless networking.

Our Focus Areas

Modeling & Measurement

Developing new models and experimental methods to understand wireless networks under diverse conditions.

Algorithms & Protocols

Designing efficient algorithms and protocols to improve mobile wireless networking.

Resilience & Performance

Enhancing robustness and optimizing performance in the presence of failures or abnormal events.

Applications & Architectures

Building network architectures for emerging domains such as mobile clouds, vehicle-to-vehicle communication, and the smart grid.
